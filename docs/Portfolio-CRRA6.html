5Riskless labor income can trivially be added to the problem, because its risklessness means that (in the absence of liquidity constraints) it is indistinguishable from a lump sum of extra current wealth with a value equal to the present discounted value (using the riskless rate) of the (riskless) future labor income. Of course, in practice, labor income is not riskless, but when labor income is risky the problem no longer has the tidy analytical solution described here and must be solved numerically.
